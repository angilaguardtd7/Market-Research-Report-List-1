<p><p>Membrane water and wastewater treatment encompasses various filtration technologies. Microfiltration employs membranes to remove suspended solids and larger particles, while ultrafiltration further reduces contaminants such as bacteria and macromolecules. Nanofiltration targets divalent ions and small organic molecules, providing a higher degree of purification. Reverse osmosis is the most advanced method, effectively removing nearly all dissolved solids and contaminants by using high pressure to force water through semi-permeable membranes. Together, these technologies offer scalable solutions for efficient water treatment.</p></p>
