<p><p>The anti-static device market is categorized into two primary types: Direct Current (DC) and Alternating Current (AC). DC anti-static devices generate a steady voltage to neutralize static electricity, making them suitable for sensitive electronic environments. In contrast, AC anti-static devices use alternating voltage to create a more dynamic charge neutralization, often preferred in industrial settings. Both types aim to prevent static discharge, protecting sensitive components and enhancing safety in various applications, including manufacturing and electronics.</p></p>
