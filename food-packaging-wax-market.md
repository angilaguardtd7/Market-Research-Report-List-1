<p><p>The food packaging wax market includes two primary types: natural wax and mineral wax. Natural wax, derived from plant sources like beeswax or carnauba, offers biodegradability and is favored for its eco-friendly properties. It enhances food preservation by providing moisture and oxygen barriers. In contrast, mineral wax, such as paraffin, is synthetically produced and known for its durability and moisture resistance. This type is often more cost-effective and widely used in traditional food packaging, catering to various industrial needs.</p></p>
